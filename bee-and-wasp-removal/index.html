Signs You May Need Professional Bee and Wasp Removal

Recognizing the early signs of a bee or wasp infestation can prevent the situation from escalating into a major problem. If you observe any of the following signs, it's time to consider professional bee and wasp removal services in Lakewood, WA:

Increased Bee or Wasp Activity

A noticeable increase in the number of bees or wasps flying around your property, particularly in specific areas, is a strong indicator that a nest is nearby. While seeing a few bees on flowers is normal and beneficial, a constant stream of insects entering or leaving a particular spot strongly suggests the presence of a colony. This elevated activity is a direct sign of an established nest, and the insects are busily going back and forth, gathering resources or tending to their young. The frequency and volume of insect traffic can provide clues about the size and maturity of the nest. Observing where they are coming from and going to can help pinpoint the location of the nest, which is often hidden from plain sight. Don't dismiss a sudden surge in activity as a temporary annoyance; it's likely a sign of something more significant happening on your property that requires attention from professionals skilled in bee and wasp removal.

Visible Nests or Hives

Discovering a visible nest is an undeniable sign of an infestation. Depending on the species, nests can vary greatly in appearance and location. Paper wasp nests are often umbrella-shaped and attached to eaves or railings. Yellow jackets frequently build their nests underground, in wall voids, or in cavities. Hornets construct large, football-shaped nests, often in trees or on buildings. While some nests are easy to spot, others can be hidden within bushes, under decks, or inside structural elements of your home. Seeing these structures is a clear indication that you have a bee or wasp problem that needs to be addressed professionally. Attempting to disturb or remove a visible nest on your own is highly dangerous and can provoke aggressive defensive behavior from the insects. Buzzing Sounds within Walls or Ceilings

Hearing persistent buzzing or scratching sounds coming from within your walls, ceilings, or attic is a common sign of an insect or animal infestation. If you suspect bees or wasps, the sounds are often consistent with the activity of a colony building or maintaining their nest within a void. These sounds can indicate a nest that is hidden and growing, where direct visual confirmation is not possible. The intensity of the buzzing might increase during warmer parts of the day when the insects are more active. Yellow Jackets

Yellow jackets are one of the most common and aggressive wasp species in the United States, including the Lakewood area. They are easily recognizable by their bright yellow and black markings and thin waist. Yellow jackets are ground nesters, often building their colonies in old rodent burrows, under porches or sheds, or in wall voids. Their colonies can grow quite large, with thousands of individuals, making them a significant threat. Disturbing a yellow jacket nest can result in a swarm of agitated wasps, posing a serious risk of multiple stings. Removing the primary bee or wasp infestation is often the first step in addressing any resulting secondary pest issues.
